High level roadmap

  • New students start on an introductory program before starting their martial arts journey. Generally, they will learn basics, slow kicks, fast kicks, paper kicks, pad strikes, marching basics, forms, self-defense, and eventually weapons and sparring. In addition to learning physical skills, they will also learn life skills such as respect, focus, and discipline.
  • White belts and Gold belts are usually in the same class. They focus on basics, respect, discipline, and focus.
  • Green belts start sparring classes and continue form classes. Sparring classes are denoted by an asterisk (*) on your school's schedule. Students must purchase a sparring equipment package from their school's director/instructor. Students must attend at least one regular class and one sparring class per week. I would recommend two of each per week if you can, we don't charge extra.
  • Purple belts continue form and sparring classes. This is when my students usually really take off since they've been a student for almost a year now.
  • Blue belts at most schools start training with the bo staff during their form class. Bo staves must be purchased from your school's director/instructor. Training with a weapon teaches the students how to treat the bo as an extension to their body.
  • Red belts start training for black belt. They are now an advanced belt, so this is the start of perfecting everything that they have learned so far. They will learn a few new things such as a new form, Zang Do Moon, but they learned most of our basics already at this point.
  • Brown belts will learn a new bo form, Discovery, and some schools teach a musical form, Beethoven. Brown belt is really just months of training for Black Belt.
  • First Degree Black belts are masters of the basics. This is where the curriculum expands immensely. Sparring competition is different, form has new weapons, they can create their own forms, self-defense has more concepts and defenses, etc.
  • Second Degree Black belts are straight up impressive martial artists. I make my 2nd degrees teach as well which comes with a huge list of skills that they pick up.
  • Third Degree Black belts are good.

Quick notes

Here are a few things that I want to get out there. Please ask me or your instructor if you have any questions on any of these points. These are all things we offer that I maybe didn't cover above.

  • Sparring equipment must be purchased from your NK school.
  • Bo staves (blue belt), kamas (black belt) and bokkens (black belt) must be purchased from your NK school.
  • Mr G's opinion: Students should bring all of their equipment to every class.
  • Cleaning your sparring equipment is mandatory.
  • A bo staff is a weapon so handle it with care and respect.
  • I never let siblings spar. There's too much that can go wrong.
  • During the school year, students must wear their full uniform to form classes. For summer classes and all sparring classes, students can either wear their full uniform or they can wear their karate pants, their belt, and a NK tshirt tucked into their pants. Some schools make it mandatory to wear the tshirt from that year.
  • Different schools have slightly different rules when it comes to upgrading from the white uniform to the white and black uniform with their name on the back. Talk to your directory to see how your school operates. There's also a blue uniform with red trim and a white uniform with red trim that you can ask about.

Tournaments

All NK students have the option of attending I think 7 tournaments that are locally run throughout the tournament season. Anyone who is a blue belt or higher has probably already heard about the NK intramural/NK team tournament and/or Diamond Nationals.

  • Just like there are ring(s) in the classroom, there will be a bunch of rings at the tournament.
  • Each ring will only have students of a certain age, gender, and belt rank.
  • We'll tell you which ring and at what time to go to your ring.
  • Students have a wide array of options that they can compete or participate in. Talk to your instructor to see which of these divisions is a good fit for your student. Also, not every tournament offers every single division. I usually recommend competing in competitive form, competitive weapons, and sparring if the student is a high enough belt for all those divisions.
    • Performance Basics is where your student and their fellow competitors get called up one at a time to the center of the ring. The center judge will read off all their moves that they practice in class and the student just has to do them (well). The students do not receive a grade/score for performance divisions.
    • Competitive Basics is where your student and their fellow competitors get called up one at a time to the center of the ring. The center judge will read off all their moves that they practice in class and the student just has to do them well. The students do receive a grade/score for performance divisions.
    • Performance Form is where your student and their fellow competitors get called up one at a time to present their form. I recommend this for anyone who has never competed before (usually white, gold, and green belts.) The students do not receive a grade/score for performance divisions.
    • Competitive Form is where your student and their fellow competitors get called up one at a time to present their form. They absolutely do get scored on their performance.
    • Competitive Weapons is just like form, but it's with weapons. This is Mrs. G's favorite competition.
    • Sparring divisions are split into a bracket where students must win in order to move on in the bracket.
    • CMX stands for creative, musical, and extreme which is usually just for black belts. Some schools have their students start musical at brown belt.
  • Here are the tournament dates for the rest of the 2025 tournament season.
    • Diamond Nationals is Friday and Saturday, October 10th and 11th.
    • NCKA Finals is Sunday, November 16th.
