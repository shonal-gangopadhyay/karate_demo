Gold belt

What to focus on these belts: key concepts, expectations, resources, and what's coming next.

Introduction

At gold belt, we introduce form into their curriculum. Form is a set of moves in a row that tell the story of a fight. The beginning forms are some boring fights, but as they progress through the ranks, the forms get better. We teach traditional taekwondo forms eventually as well as creative and extreme forms (imagine Hollywood fight scenes). Forms help the students with coupling the memorization of the moves to motions with their body, exercising multiple muscle sets, thinking and moving simultaneously, performing by themselves, and more. I could talk about form all day.

Expectations

  • Bring uniform and belt; arrive 5 minutes early. Ask an instructor if you need help tying the belt.
  • Students turn in an attendance card to class. We keep track of their attendance as well as their progress using these cards.

Equipment & Safety

At this rank, the students only need their uniform. The uniform must be a National Karate uniform. During the summer, students may wear a National Karate t-shirt tucked into their pants accompanied by their belt instead of the full uniform. At gold belt with a green stripe (aka green stripe), I would recommend talking to Mr. Boone about purchasing sparring equipment which they will start using at solid green belt.

The way tape stripes work

The electrical tape that we use on the students' belts is for keeping track of their skill as well as their attendance. Every 4(ish) classes, we take a look at the students in class to see how they're doing in regards to the karate moves and as well as their listening skills. Depending on that, we award them with a tape. When they earn all three tapes, they are pretty close to testing for their next belt.

Tournaments

We do offer tournaments at this level to the students. Just like there are four rings in the main classroom, there are a bunch of rings at the tournaments. The student's assigned ring will be with other students their age and belt level. They can't compete in every division since they can't backflip (yet), but they can participate in the following divisions:

  • Performance Basics is where they get called up one at a time to do the basic karate moves that we do in class. Many time before a tournament, all we do is practice these moves in order to get them ready for the tournaments. In this division, it's like a participation division where everyone gets a trophy. In my opinion, this is a great division for new competitors and it should also be paired with the competitive basics division, so the students compete in both divisions. Additionally, it's been a while since your student has done a tournament, you can treat this division as the warm up for the competitive division.
  • Competitive Basics is where they get called up one at a time to do the basic moves, but this times it's graded and compared to the rest of the students. Here's what the judges grade on:
    • Correct Technique (i.e. twist on a punch)
    • Speed (move as fast as you can on every single motion)
    • Power (hit hard)
    • Loud Yells (yell loudly and no screaming)
    • Intense/Focused Face (intense face and look at your target instead of around the room)
  • Performance Form is where they get called up one at a time to present their form. We frequently practice the routine in classes leading up to tournaments. In this division, it's like a participation division where everyone gets a trophy. In my opinion, this is a great division for new competitors and it should also be paired with the competitive form division, so the students compete in both divisions. Additionally, if it has been a while since your student has done a tournament, you can treat this division as the warm up for the competitive division.
  • Competitive Form is where they get called up one at a time to their form, but this time it's graded and compared to the rest of the students. Here's what the judges grade on:
    • Correct Technique (i.e. correct and deep stances)
    • Speed (move as fast as you can on every single motion)
    • Power (hit hard)
    • Loud Yells (yell loudly and no screaming)
    • Intense/Focused Face (intense face and look at your target instead of around the room)

What's next

The next belt is green belt. At green belt, students start the sparring class. They will need a set of sparring equipment at this rank, so please talk to Mr. Boone while they are still a gold belt with a green stripe.
